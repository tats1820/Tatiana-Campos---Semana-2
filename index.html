Totoro

Forest spirits living inside a camphor tree

My Neighbor Totoro is one of the best known animated films, which although at first was not very successful, it is now considered a classic. A little recap for those who don't know the movie: it's about two girls, Satsuki and Mei, who move to the countryside with their family and come in contact with Totoro, a forest spirit who will guide them through the wonderful world of the forest and its hidden wonders. But what is Totoro? Not even its director is clear, "I think he lives on acorns. He's supposedly the protector of the forest, but it's just a half-baked idea, an approximation." Physically he looks like a mixture of several animals; rabbit, cat and owl -because he flies-. Although the most popular theory is that Totoro is actually a troll. Yes, a friendly and cuddly troll. Why is this said? Well, there are several clues, pay attention. The first is that the name "Totoro" is actually a modification of the word "Tor≈çru," which means troll in Japanese. It could be that Mei, one of the sisters, when she meets Totoro called him that precisely alluding to the mythological creature, but since she is a little girl, she mispronounces it. In addition, there is a part of the film in which the mother is shown reading a story to the two girls. From the cover, we know that this story is about The Three Male Gruff Goats, a Norwegian fairy tale in which three goats cross a bridge under which there is a troll who wants to eat them. Only that on the cover of the book, in the movie, the troll is replaced by an image of Totoro. This is more than enough evidence to confirm what was already suspected at the beginning.
